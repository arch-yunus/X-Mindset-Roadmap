# ?? 02_The_Algorithm

> **"If you aren't adding back at least 10% of what you deleted, you're not deleting enough."**

The Algorithm is a rigid, five-step engineering protocol designed to maximize production efficiency and system reliability by eliminating waste at the foundational level.

---

## ?? Step-by-Step Execution Protocol

### 1. Question Every Requirement
Each requirement must be attached to a specific person who can defend it. If the owner of the requirement can't explain why it exists, it must be deleted. A person's name is required because you cannot question a "department" or a "committee."

### 2. Delete the Part/Process
Aggressively remove components or steps from the manufacturing or software process. If you aren't forced to add something back occasionally, your deletion margin is too conservative. **Bias toward deletion.**

### 3. Simplify or Optimize
Never optimize something that should not exist in the first place. This is a common trap—engineers spend months optimizing a part that should have been deleted in Step 2.

### 4. Accelerate Cycle Time
Increase the velocity of the process. If it's slow, figure out the bottleneck and eliminate it. However, you must *only* do this after the first three steps have been completed.

### 5. Automate
The final step. Automation without simplification is just automating a mess. Never automate something that hasn't been through the first four steps of the algorithm.

---

## ?? Implementation Metrics
- **Deletion Yield:** Percentage of parts/processes removed.
- **Cycle Acceleration:** Reduction in time-to-output.
- **Requirement Source Integrity:** 100% of requirements mapped to verifiable owners.
